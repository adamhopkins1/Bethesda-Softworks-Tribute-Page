A Tribute to one of Maryland's best game studios:Bethesda Softworks.

A Celebration of 35 years of gaming excellence.

A Timeline of the Journey of a small studio in Montgomery County,Maryland that went from a PC Developer to becoming a monolith of game development:

  • Pre-Bethesda:Christopher Weaver: "The founder of Bethesda Softworks Christopher Weaver was a engineer who worked in the television and cable industries. His first job was at ABC where he conceptualized the idea of broadband and satelites becoming an integral part of network television essentially visualizing the future of streaming as being the future of television which ABC hires him to becoming a tech forcaster."
  • Pre-Bethesda:Christopher Weaver Pt.2: "Christopher Weaver leaves leaves ABC to join the National Cable Television Association and created a dept dedicated to advancing technology to improve Cable television. He later became a part of the government as a member of the United States House Subcommitee on Communications as their chief enginneer where he helped to contribute to improving or regulating communication through legislation"
  • Pre-Bethesda:Christopher Weaver pt.3: "Weaver founds VideoMagic with a friend from MIT the company starts out making Location based enterntainment but it doesn't pan out due to lack of commercial interest. Christopher Weaver would later abondon the VideoMagic Concept and create another company 'Media Technology Associates, Limited' or 'Media Technology Limited in 1981' The company provided consulting to private companies and government organizations."
  • Pre-Bethesda:Christopher Weaver pt.4: "Christopher Weaver would meet Ed Fletcher an former employee he worked with at VideoMagic on a few video games for the now-defunct LaserDisc player. Fletcher would suggest that the two make a video game based on Football due to the fact that Ed Fletcher was a big football fan. Christopher would agree and go along with it and the two would develop Gridiron! the precursor to the Madden Franchise of video games from Electronic Arts."
  • 1986:The Birth of Bethesda Softworks "The Company which was originally an testing ground to see if PC games were a viable market the company would release Gridiron! for the Amiga, Atari ST, and Commodore64 systems."
  • 1990: "The company would move from Bethesda to Rockville MD,And would release their first game The Terminator on MS-DOS in 1991 to coincide with the release of the sequel Terminator 2:Judgement Day. "
  • 1994:The Beginning of Elder Scrolls and the Seed of Starfield "The company would release their first game in the The Elder Scrolls:Arena series of Elder Scrolls video games. The game was originally concieved by Julian Lefay and Vijay Lakshman who were programmer and director and producer respecively at the same year the company was working on 'The 10th Planet' which was a collaboration between Bethesda Softworks and Action filmmaker Roland Emmerich famous for films like Independence Day. The Project fell thorugh but would be the nucleus of the upcoming Bethesda Softworks video game 'Starfield'.
  • 1995: "The Company would make its first acquisition in Flashpoint Productions which created mostly racing games and would be named Mediatech West"
  • 1997: "Bethesda would make a second acquisition in XL translab an graphics company that was later moved to Bethesda Softworks Main Headquarters which would make Bethesda Sofworks the third biggest company in the PC gaming industry after LucasArts and Interplay.
    • 1997:pt2 "Bethesda would release their second Elder Scrolls video game 'Daggerfall' using their first custom in-house 3D engine the XnGine which would also be the engine which would be the genesis of their future engines such as the Creation Engine. The XnGine would be later used in legacy Bethesda Softworks titles such as 'The Termninator:Future Shock' in the same year Bethesda would release the spin off Elder Scrolls:Battlespire"
  • 1998:Bethesda find its wunderkind in Todd Howard "In 1998 Todd Howard directs his first Elder Scrolls game In 'The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard'.Todd Howard would be an essential component and element to the success of the Mainline Elder Scrolls games and Fallout series of video games.'Redguard ' would be the last game to use the Xngine and unforntuately 'Redguard' did not do commercially well.
  • 1999:Enter Pete Hines and ZeniMax Media. "Pete Hines joined Bethesda Softworks in its Marketing department he would also be instrumental in how he helped promote Bethesda Softworks video games and spearheading campaigns for many of its titles reacing beyond the confines of PC gaming and into mainstream gaming. in this same year Christopher Weaver and Robert A. Altman (no relation to the Film Director) would form the holding company ZeniMax Media after aquiring Media Technology and thus becoming one unit company.
  • 2001:The Christining of Bethesda Game Studios "Bethesda Game Studios is established and is an Bethesda Softworks becomes a publishing branch of ZeniMax Media."
  • 2002:Christopher Weaver Leaves Bethesda. "The longtime founder of Bethesda was no longer an employee of ZeniMax after the ZeniMax acquisition he sues ZeniMax for 1.2 million and amongst claims that he was ousted by ZeniMax and wanted his severance pay. The parties of Christopher Weaver and ZeniMax would later settle out of court. But Weaver would still be a shareholder in the company."
  • 2007:Bethesda Softworks Acquires the Fallout IP And The Rest is History. "Bethesda Softworks would acquire the Fallout IP from Interplay Enternatinment. Bethesda Game Studio would begin the development of Fallout 3."
  • 2008 "Fallout 3 is released in 2008 and would include 5 expansions such as Operation:Anchorage, The Pitt, Broken Steel, Point Lookout, and Mother Ship Zeta this would be the beginning of the many different games in the series such as Fallout:New Vegas, Fallout 4 and Fallout:76.Fallout 3 would prove to be a massive commericial and critical success garnering numerous Game of the Year awards and critical praise."
  • 2009 "Bethesda Softworks would enter litigation against Interplay due to the latter not meeting obligations with Bethesda for the Fallout MMO game which would later be made as Fallout:76. Bethesda would stop funding the Interplay developed Fallout MMO and force Interplay to stop working on it.Bethesda Softworks would make its biggest acquisition yet in Famed FPS Developer Id Software makers of such titles as DOOM, QUAKE,Wolfenstein and at the time their newest IP RAGE."
  • 2016 "Bethesda's Acqusition of Id Software would bare fruit in the release of the DOOM Reboot the first made without the original creators John Romero and John Carmack involvement it would be a critical and commercial success for Id software after many failed attempts to reboot DOOM and having less than suceesful games under publisher Activision DOOM would prove to be another successful story for Bethesda Softworks."
  • 2018 "Bethesda would release Fallout:76 the studios attempt to salvage the failed MMO project that Interplay worked on would now fall on Bethesda Softworks to complete as many would've expected the game would recieve mixed to negative reviews for its poor implementation,quality and bugs the project would also contain micro-transactions. Bethesda would continue supporting Fallout:76 for many years eventually entering into a partership with Double Eleven a studio founded by former Rockstar Leeds Developers in 2022. in 2018 Bethesda would release Rage 2 and Doom Eternal the sequel to the DOOM 2016 reboot."
  • 2021:A Move that Shakes the Foundations of Western Game Development "In March 2021 ZeniMax was acquired by Microsoft Corporation for $7.5 Billion dollars and is now a a subsiderary of Xbox Game Studios thus fulfilling the original dream of Christopher Weaver of having digital and streaming become the major force for the future of entertainment with Xbox Game Studios Game Pass and CEO Phil Spencer's vision of making gaming for all this acquisition was a moment in gaming that established the firstfruits of that vision of a connected future where gaming can be for everyone and not just for the limited few.Gaming can be as powerful and impactful as any other entertainment medium and be just as exciting. What started from a kitchen table in Christopher Weaver's home grew to become what is undoubtedly a powerhouse of Western Game development and Be the beacon of the future of Game Development overall."

“We’re headed in the right direction. I want us to be seen as the developers that keep that old school game experience at heart, but keeps pushing it forward, that tries new things. If you see ‘Bethesda Game Studios’ on the box, you know there are some crazy ideas in there. We won’t always get it right, but we’ll always keep trying.”

--Christopher Weaver, Founder of Bethesda Softworks
